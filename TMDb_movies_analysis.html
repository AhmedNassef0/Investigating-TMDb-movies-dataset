Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue. Certain columns, like castand genres, contain multiple values separated by pipe (|) characters.There are some odd characters in the cast column. Dont worry about cleaning them. You can leave them as is. The final two columns ending with _adj show the budget and revenue of the associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars, accounting for inflation over time.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  • What is the mean and distribution of vote average?
  • Which moviehas the shortest/longest runtime?
  • Which year has the most movies released?
  • Which movie has the highest/lowest profit?
  • Most Frequent movie director?
  • What is production company with the most profit?
  • Does the production company affects on movie's popularity?
  • Which genres are most popular from year to year?
  • The relationship between vote average and revenue

    #Make function to find min and max
def minmax(x):
    #use the function 'idmin' to find the index of lowest profit movie.
    min_movie = df[x].idxmin()
    #use the function 'idmax' to find the index of Highest profit movie.
    max_movie = df[x].idxmax()
    high = pd.DataFrame(df.loc[max_movie,:])
    low = pd.DataFrame(df.loc[min_movie,:])
    print("Movie Which Has Highest "+ x + " : ",df['original_title'][max_movie])
    print("Movie Which Has Lowest "+ x + "  : ",df['original_title'][min_movie])
    return pd.concat([high,low],axis = 1)
